Coverage

What's Covered

Your protection plan covers losses caused by the following events.

Burglary

Forced entry theft with police report*

Building Collapse

Including weight of snow, ice, or windstorm

Stored Vehicles

Damage to stored vehicle, boat, or trailer

Fire

Fire damage to your stored belongings

Water Damage

Including mold and mildew coverage

Rodent & Vermin

Damage caused by rodents or vermin

Vandalism

Intentional damage to your property

*Evidence of forced entry and police report required for burglary claims. Some coverages have additional limits—please contact us for details. Water damage does not extend to flood or surface water.

Exclusions

What's Not Covered

The following items and events are not covered under the protection plan.

Excluded Items

Refrain from storing the following goods as they are not covered:

  • Currency, documents, records, money, notes, or securities
  • Aircraft
  • Animals, birds, or fish
  • Antiques
  • Contraband or property held for illegal purposes
  • Firearms
  • Furs and fur garments
  • Jewelry, watches, precious stones, or metals
  • Valuable papers and electronic data

Notable Plan Exclusions

The following events are not covered under any plan:

  • Flood
  • Surface water
  • Mysterious disappearance

This is a summary. Please refer to your self-storage rental agreement for a full description of terms, conditions, limits, and exclusions.

Best Practices

Tips for Storing Your Property

These practices will help you prepare for the unexpected.

Document Your Items

Keep an inventory of your items and take photographs before storing them.

Cover Your Property

Use drop cloths or plastic covers to protect your belongings from dust and debris.

Elevate Your Boxes

Place boxes on pallets, skids, or 2x4's to keep them off the ground.

Use a Quality Lock

Always lock your unit with a cylinder or disc padlock for maximum security.

Protect Electronics

Store electronics in the rear of the unit, away from the door opening.

Know Your Plan

Avoid storing items that are not protected by your plan to prevent uncovered losses.

Claims Process

How to File a Claim

Here's what to do immediately after you become aware of damage or theft.

1

Take Photos

Images of the damage will help resolve the claim even faster.

2

Protect Your Property

Take immediate action to protect your property from further damage.

3

Keep Evidence

Retain your lock and damaged property—we'll need these to resolve the claim.

4

Call the Police

Report break-ins to local police and obtain a copy of the report.

5

Provide Proof of Value

Sales receipts, photos, and other evidence of loss will assist in handling your claim.

6

Submit Online

File your claim at safelease.com/claims.

Information Required to Submit a Claim
