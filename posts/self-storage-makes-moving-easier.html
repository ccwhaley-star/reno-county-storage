Why Self Storage Makes Moving Easier

Take the stress out of your next move with a flexible storage solution.

Home › Blog › Why Self Storage Makes Moving Easier
Moving Tips March 16, 2026 11 min read

Moving is consistently ranked as one of life’s most stressful events — right up there with starting a new job or going through a major life change. Whether you’re relocating to Hutchinson for work, downsizing after the kids leave home, or just moving across Reno County to be closer to family, the logistics of packing, hauling, and unpacking can feel overwhelming. But here’s the good news: a self storage unit can take a huge amount of pressure off your shoulders and turn a chaotic move into something manageable. We see it all the time at Reno County Storage, and the people who use mini storage during their move almost always say the same thing — they wish they’d done it sooner.

Let’s break down the different ways self storage makes moving easier, answer the questions we hear most often from new tenants, and explain why our facility on E 17th Ave is built for exactly this kind of situation.

Bridge the Gap Between Homes

If you’ve ever moved before, you know that move-out and move-in dates almost never line up perfectly. Maybe your lease ends on the 1st but you can’t get into your new place until the 15th. Maybe your home sale closed faster than expected, and your new house isn’t ready for another three weeks. That gap can feel like a crisis if you don’t have a plan.

A self storage unit on E 17th Ave gives you a safe, secure place to keep everything during that in-between period. Instead of scrambling to find a friend’s garage, stuffing everything into a relative’s basement, or paying for an extra month of rent at a place you’ve already left, you can load your belongings into a storage unit and breathe easy. Your stuff stays dry, locked up, and waiting for you whenever you’re ready.

We hear this story a lot from folks moving within the Hutchinson area. Someone sells their house near downtown Hutchinson, buys a place out closer to Carey Park, and needs two weeks of overlap storage. A mini storage unit handles that perfectly — no stress, no begging for favors, and no risk of your couch sitting in someone’s driveway getting rained on.

Can I rent month-to-month? Absolutely. Every lease at Reno County Storage is month-to-month. There are no long-term contracts and no penalties for leaving whenever you’re done. If you only need storage for two weeks between homes, you pay for one month and move out when you’re ready. It’s that simple.

Declutter Before You Pack

Here’s a moving tip that professional organizers swear by: don’t try to pack everything at once. Moving is the perfect time to sort through your belongings, and a self storage unit gives you a place to stage things as you go.

Start a few weeks before your move date. Go room by room and pull out the items you know you want to keep but don’t need right away — seasonal decorations, holiday supplies, camping gear, that treadmill you keep meaning to use. Load those into your storage unit first. Then tackle the rarely-used furniture, the boxes of books, and anything else that’s taking up space but isn’t part of your daily life.

By moving these items into mini storage early, you clear out space in your current home. That makes it easier to pack what’s left, easier to clean, and easier to see what you actually have. A lot of people discover during this process that they own way more than they realized — and having that storage unit means you don’t have to make snap decisions about what to keep and what to donate. You can sort through things at your own pace after the move.

If you’re not sure what size unit you need for a partial load of household items, check out our size guide. A 5×10 or 10×10 unit handles the early-stage decluttering for most families, and you can always upgrade later if you need more room.

Stage Your Home for Sale

If you’re selling your home in the Hutchinson area, real estate agents will tell you the same thing: a clean, spacious-looking house sells faster and for more money. Buyers want to walk into a room and picture their own furniture there — not navigate around your overstuffed bookshelf and collection of family photos.

Moving extra furniture, personal items, and general clutter into a self storage unit is one of the easiest and most affordable ways to improve your home’s appeal on the Reno County market. You don’t need to hire a professional stager. Just remove about a third of the furniture from each room, clear off the countertops, and box up anything personal. The difference is dramatic, and it costs next to nothing compared to a price reduction on your listing.

This is especially useful for folks selling older homes near downtown Hutchinson or in the neighborhoods around the Kansas Cosmosphere. Those homes have a ton of charm, but the rooms tend to run smaller than what buyers expect today. Clearing out extra furniture makes them feel bigger and brighter, which translates directly to better offers.

How quickly can I move in? You can reserve a unit online and move in the same day. There’s no waiting period, no approval process, and no appointment needed. If your real estate agent tells you on Monday that you need to declutter before the open house on Saturday, you can have a storage unit loaded by Wednesday.

Move at Your Own Pace

Not everyone can afford to hire a moving crew for a one-day blitz. And honestly, not everyone wants to. Plenty of people prefer to move gradually — a truckload on Saturday, a few carloads during the week, and the big furniture pieces when you can round up some help.

With a self storage unit just off Highway 50, you can move on your own schedule. Our facility at 2511 E 17th Ave has drive-up access on every unit, so you can back a truck or trailer right up to your door and load at your own pace. No elevators, no hallways, no dollies — just a straight shot from your vehicle to your unit.

This approach works especially well for families. If you’re juggling kids, work schedules, and everything else that comes with daily life in Reno County, trying to do a full move in one day is a recipe for exhaustion and arguments. Give yourself permission to take a week or two. Move the garage stuff to storage on a weekday evening. Bring the kitchen boxes over on Saturday morning before heading to the Kansas State Fair. Handle the bedrooms next weekend. There’s no rush when you have a storage unit holding everything safely.

What size do I need for moving? For a full household move, most families use a 10×15 or 10×20 unit. A 10×20 holds roughly the contents of a three-bedroom house. If you’re just storing overflow items or doing a partial move, a 10×10 usually does the trick. Our size guide has a full breakdown with room-by-room estimates.

Downsizing Made Simple

Downsizing is one of the most common reasons people rent self storage in Hutchinson. Maybe the kids have moved out and you’re trading your four-bedroom house for a two-bedroom place closer to town. Maybe you’re retiring and simplifying. Whatever the reason, moving to a smaller home means you’ll have more stuff than space — at least at first.

A mini storage unit lets you keep the items you’re not ready to part with while you settle into your new place and figure out what actually fits. That antique dresser from your grandmother? Keep it in storage until you know whether it works in the new bedroom. The kids’ sports equipment they might want when they come home for the holidays? Store it. The patio furniture that won’t fit on your new deck? Give yourself time to decide.

The worst thing you can do when downsizing is rush into getting rid of things you might regret later. Storage gives you breathing room. Use it for a few months while you adjust, and then gradually move things out of storage as you figure out your new normal. Sell what you don’t need, donate what you can, and keep what matters — all on your own timeline.

Is there a deposit? Nope. At Reno County Storage, there’s no security deposit, no move-in fee, and no hidden charges. You pay your first month’s rent, get a free lock, and you’re in. That’s it. When you’re already spending money on a move, the last thing you need is surprise fees.

New to Hutchinson? Welcome!

If you’re moving to Reno County from out of the area — whether it’s for a job, family, military reassignment, or just because you fell in love with small-town Kansas life — a self storage unit can be your first stop. Reserve online before you even arrive, drop off your belongings when you get to town, and take your time finding the right home.

Hutchinson is a great place to land. You’ve got the Kansas Cosmosphere for history, Dillon Nature Center for hiking and fishing, the Hutchinson Zoo for culture, and a downtown square that still feels like the heart of a real community. The Hutchinson Recreation Commission has programs for families, and the local schools are solid. It’s the kind of town where people wave from their front porches and actually mean it. If you’re still getting your bearings, our guide to moving to Hutchinson covers neighborhoods, cost of living, schools, and local resources worth knowing about.

Our facility is conveniently located at 2511 E 17th Ave, with easy access from Highway 50 — so whether you’re coming from Wichita, Dodge City, or points beyond, you can find us without any trouble. And if you’re active military or a veteran, we offer a 10% military discount as a thank-you for your service. Reno County Storage is veteran-owned through Midwest Storage Solutions LLC, so supporting the military community is personal for us.

Can I share a unit with someone? Yes. If you’re moving to Hutchinson with a roommate, partner, or family member and want to split a unit, that’s perfectly fine. Just keep in mind that the person on the lease is the one responsible for the unit. We recommend keeping an inventory of who owns what — it keeps things simple if your situation changes later.
